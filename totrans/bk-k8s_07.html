PART II

CONTAINERS IN KUBERNETES

Computers have finite processing, storage, and memory, and are built of parts that fail, especially at the wrong time. To build a scalable, reliable application, we can’t be limited by the resources of a single host or dependent on a single point of failure. At the same time, we don’t want to give up the modularity and flexibility that containers provide. In Part II, we’ll see how Kubernetes meets the essential requirements to run containers across a cluster of machines, with cross-host container networking, scalability, automated failover, and distributed storage.
